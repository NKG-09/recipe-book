Pasta

Nice photo of your soon to be pasta.

Description

This pasta is a delicious, comforting dish that’s perfect for any occasion. The tomato sauce is rich, fresh, and full of flavor, with a touch of garlic, basil, and olive oil. It’s a simple and hearty meal that can be made in about 30 minutes, making it ideal for both a weeknight dinner or a weekend treat.

The pasta perfectly complements the robust sauce, and you can easily customize this dish by adding grated cheese or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes if you like a little spice.

Ingredients

For pasta

  • 400g (14oz) pasta
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for boiling pasta)

For tomato sauce

  • 6 ripe tomatoes (or one 400g can of crushed tomatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil (or 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to package instructions (usually around 9-11 minutes), until al dente. Drain and set aside, reserving a small cup of pasta water for later.

  2. Make the tomato sauce

    • If using fresh tomatoes, blanch them in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then remove the skins. Chop the tomatoes and set them aside. If using canned crushed tomatoes, you can skip this step.
    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes, until soft and translucent.
    • Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds, until fragrant.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes (or canned crushed tomatoes) to the skillet, along with oregano, basil, sugar, and salt & pepper to taste. Stir to combine.
    • Let the sauce simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld together.

  3. Combine pasta and sauce

    • Once the sauce is ready, add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat the pasta in the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little reserved pasta water to loosen it up.

  4. Serve

    • Plate the pasta and garnish with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.

Enjoy your delicious pasta! This dish is simple yet packed with flavor, perfect for a satisfying meal.
